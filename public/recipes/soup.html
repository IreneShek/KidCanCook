Cream of Tomato Soup



Reference: recipet from "bon appetit"

Ingredient

  • 2 Tbsp unsalted butter (or vegetable oil)
  • 1/2 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 5 whole Beef Tomatoes, cut in quarters
  • 1 Tbsp dry Basil
  • 4 Tbsp cup (or more) heavy cream
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper

Ingredient for Additional flavor

  • 2 Tbsp tomato paste or 1 cup chicken stock
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper or paprika

Recipe Preparation

  1. Melt butter in a large heavy pot over medium heat. Add thyme, onion, and garlic. Cook until onion is completely soft and translucent, 10–12 minutes. Increase heat to medium-high; add tomato paste. Continue cooking, stirring often, until paste has begun to caramelize in spots, 5–6 minutes.
  2. Add tomatoes with juices, 1 tsp. sugar, and 8 cups water to pot. Increase heat to high; bring to a simmer. Reduce heat to medium. Simmer until flavors meld and soup reduces to about 2 quarts (8 cups), 45–55 minutes. Remove soup from heat; let cool slightly. Discard thyme sprigs. Working in small batches, purée soup in a blender until smooth. Return to pot.
    DO AHEAD: Soup can be made 2 days ahead. Let cool slightly, cover, and chill. Rewarm before continuing.
  3. Stir in ¼ cup cream. Simmer soup until flavors meld, 10–15 minutes longer. Season to taste with salt, pepper, and remaining 1 tsp. sugar. Add more cream, if desired.

Tips:

  • Unsalted butter can be replace with vegetable oil or Oilve oil.
  • Basil can be replace with any kind of dry/fresh herbs.
  • Heavy cream can be replace with milk for lighter texture.

Cream of Mushroom Soup



Reference: recipet from "Taste of Home"

Ingredient

  • 2 Tbsp butter
  • 1/2 pound sliced fresh mushrooms
  • 1/2 medium chopped onion
  • 6 Tbsp all-purpose flour
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup half-and-half cream

Ingredient for Additional flavor

  • 2 cups of grated hard cheese

Recipe Preparation

  1. In a large saucepan, heat butter over medium-high heat; saute mushrooms and onion until tender.
  2. Mix flour, salt, pepper and 1 can broth until smooth; stir into mushroom mixture. Stir in remaining can of broth. Bring to a boil; cook and stir until thickened, about 2 minutes. Reduce heat; stir in cream. Simmer, uncovered, until flavors are blended, about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Tips:

  • Many people don't know the difference between chicken broth and chicken stock. Chicken stock is made from bone, while chicken broth is made from meat.
  • Mushrooms are rich in vitamin B, which makes them a great supporter of heart health.
  • Heavy cream can be replace with milk for lighter texture.

Cream of Potato Soup



Reference: recipet from "Taste of Home"

Ingredient

  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 Tbsp chopped onion
  • 2 Tbsp butter
  • 2 Tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper

Ingredient for Additional flavor

  • Paprika and minced fresh parsley
  • 1/8 tsp celery salt
  • 1 cup of cooked corn/carrot/peas

Recipe Preparation

  1. Place the potatoes and water in a saucepan; bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Cover and cook until tender; drain and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, saute onion in butter until tender. Stir in flour until blended. Gradually stir in milk. Bring to a boil; cook and stir for 2 minutes or until thickened. Reduce heat; add the potatoes. Cook for 2-3 minutes or until heated through.
  3. Optional: Sprinkle with parsley and paprika.

Tips:

  • Unsalted butter can be replace with vegetable oil or Oilve oil.
  • Can add meat at the end. Such as 1/2 cup of Bacon cut into Lardons. Or 1 cup of chicken / cooked ground beef.
  • Heavy cream can be replace with milk for lighter texture.
